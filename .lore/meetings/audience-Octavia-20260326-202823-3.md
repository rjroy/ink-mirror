## Meeting Notes: ink-mirror v1 Spec Review with Octavia

Octavia conducted a comprehensive design review of the v1-core-loop specification, examining clarity, completeness, consistency, and verifiability. The review identified five categories of findings: undefined thresholds (REQ-V1-9's "enough history" lacked forward reference to Tier 2's 5-entry threshold), incomplete success criteria (contradiction detection in REQ-V1-19 was specified but not checklistable), boundary confusion (Architecture Constraints mixed verifiable outcomes with implementation mandates), and scope gaps (undecided observation resurfacing and observation-to-rule transformation format both lacked definition). The review noted that the curation test itself requires human judgment and cannot be automated.

Five corrections were applied: REQ-V1-9 now references REQ-V1-13 explicitly, Architecture Constraints were split into Verifiable Constraints (testable outcomes) and Implementation Mandates (construction choices from the daemon-first pattern), a success criterion was added for contradiction detection, the curation test criterion was expanded and a validation note added to the AI Validation section, and both undecided resurfacing scope and observation-to-rule format were promoted to Open Questions with explicit dependency flagging.

The vision document was simultaneously refactored: all spec-level material was extracted, leaving only project identity and philosophy (the four core principles and five "what it is not" statements). The new `.lore/specs/v1-core-loop.md` contains all v1 requirements organized by phase, with profile versioning appropriately stubbed for its own specification. No content was lost; everything from the vision that belonged in specification now lives in the spec with improved structure and referenced research threads.

All notes were accounted for. Session ready to close.
