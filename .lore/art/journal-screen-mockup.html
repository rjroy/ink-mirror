The morning ran long. I had three things to do before nine and by eight forty I was still on the first one, which had split into four. There's a version of this that's just bad planning. There's another version where the thing genuinely had more surface area than it looked like from outside. I keep noticing I reach for the second explanation when the first one is probably accurate. It's more comfortable to say the work was complex than to say I underestimated it. Both can be true at once and usually are, but the ratio matters and I'm not honest about it. What I actually want: to be better at the initial surface read. Not to stop being surprised — surprises are fine — but to build a faster internal calibration. The gap between "this looks like two hours" and "this is actually a day" shouldn't take half the morning to discover.
